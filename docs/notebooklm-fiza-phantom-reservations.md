# Fiza's Phantom Reservations — Source Material

## Character
- **Auntie Fiza**: Salon owner from Karachi. Sarcastic, dramatic, dry wit. Treats every situation like a Bollywood film. High-energy when annoyed, slow dramatic delivery when exhausted. Uses Roman Urdu + English mix naturally.
- **Voice Direction**: South Asian accent (Desi/Karachi). Warm middle-aged woman. Think: that one auntie at every wedding who runs the show and has opinions about everyone's hair and life choices. Slightly exasperated, very expressive, speaks in a measured pace when delivering punchlines — NOT rushed.
- **Accent Notes**: Soft "t" and "d" sounds, occasional Urdu filler words ("acha", "beta", "bas", "dekho"), English words pronounced with Desi rhythm. When she's being sarcastic, slow down and drawl slightly.

## Core Pain Point (Restaurant Edition)
- Restaurants that let you "book" but then lose your reservation
- Showing up to find the table "actually booked" (translation: someone tipped the maître d')
- Waiting 45 minutes while the hostess says "just 5 more minutes" with a straight face
- Group of 12 showing up unannounced and getting seated before you
- The universal desi restaurant experience where "reservation" means "suggestion"

## Pabandi Solution (simple language)
- Restaurants use Pabandi to take deposits when booking
- No deposit = no reservation
- If you no-show, restaurant keeps deposit (chai money)
- Trust Score shows reliable bookers vs flakes

## Dialogue Notes (Tempo Control)
- When Fiza is **sarcastic/dramatic**: SLOW DOWN. Let her drawl. Each word should feel expensive. Like she's delivering a TED Talk on restaurant betrayal.
- When Fiza is **angry/action-oriented**: Speed up slightly, but still measured. Think "auntie who just realized she paid for someone else's biryani."
- **Slow tempo instruction for NotebookLM**: When Fiza speaks her punchlines, reduce speaking rate by ~15%. Give each sarcastic line a 0.5-second pause before the next line. The comedy comes from the SLOW BURN, not the speed.
- **Roman Urdu words**: Keep them in the script. Do not translate "beta", "biryani", "dekho", "acha", "bas", "Pehle payment phir baat". This is how desi women actually talk.

## South Asian Accent Guidance (for NotebookLM)
- Soft dental consonants: make "t" and "d" feel slightly softer than American English
- Stress pattern: English words get the Desi stress shift (resERvation → res-er-VA-tion)
- Intonation: slight sing-song rise at the end of questions
- Emotional tone: warm but world-weary, with bursts of dramatic flair
- When slow-tempo: imagine Fiza is explaining this to her best friend over chai, very deliberately, with lots of eye-roll energy
