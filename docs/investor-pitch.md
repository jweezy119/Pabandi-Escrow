# Investor Pitch — Pabandi
## Phone Call Script

---

## 30-Second Elevator Pitch

"Hi, I'm Jawad, founder of Pabandi. We're solving phantom booking and no-show problems for US and global service businesses. Right now, live sellers, real estate agents, restaurants, and freelancers waste hours chasing clients who ghost or don't show up. Pabandi requires upfront deposits and builds a portable Trust Score so businesses know who's serious before they open the door. After a 30-day free pilot, our business subscriptions start at $29/month. We're targeting global service economy workers who currently have no reliability layer."

---

## 2-Minute Detailed Pitch

**1. The Problem (0:00-0:25)**
"Service businesses globally face a massive trust gap. A restaurant owner tells me she turned away walk-ins because someone 'booked' a table for 20 people who never showed. A real estate agent spent 3 hours yesterday waiting for a client who was 'running late.' A live seller on Instagram gets 97 comments, 10 actual orders, and 3 payments. These aren't edge cases — this is daily life for millions of service workers globally."

The problem is simple: right now, there's no cost to flake. No deposit, no consequence, no reputation that travels with you."

**2. The Solution (0:25-0:55)**
"Pabandi is a booking and escrow platform built for the global service economy. We have three core mechanics:

First, upfront deposits. Before a client gets a table, a viewing, or a delivery slot, they pay a small deposit. No show? The business keeps it. Simple.

Second, portable Trust Scores. Every user gets a score based on their booking history — show-ups, cancellations, disputes. This score travels with them across every Pabandi business, so a bad actor can't just create a new account and ghost again.

Third, Pabandi Points — not crypto, because crypto isn't halal and isn't understood by the masses. Points are simple, compliant with Islamic finance principles, and usable for staking without ribā, gharar, or maysir."

**3. The Business Model (0:55-1:15)**
"We charge businesses starting at $29/month after a 30-day free pilot. No credit card required to start. If they don't get a booking delivered in 30 days, they owe us nothing. That's our promise.

Our CAC is near zero because the product sells itself — one restaurant owner tells 5 others. Our churn is low because the ROI is immediate: one missed booking costs more than a year of Pabandi. We also take a 2% platform fee on deposits, which scales with volume."

**4. Why Now / Why Us (1:15-1:40)**
"Global service sectors — live selling, freelance services, short-term rentals, hospitality — are exploding. But trust infrastructure is stuck in the past. Every other market segment has moved to digital escrow and reputation. The global service economy needs a trust layer.

"We're not building a Western app localized for another market. We're building from scratch for global service culture: multilingual support, halal-compliant options available, privacy-first, and a customer-obsessed approach.

**5. The Ask (1:40-2:00)**
"We're raising a pre-seed round to acquire our first 100 paying businesses and prove unit economics. The ask is [X amount] for [X] equity. With that, we can onboard 100 businesses, process [Y] bookings, and generate [Z] in monthly recurring revenue within 6 months. In 12 months, we're the default trust layer for the global informal service economy."

## Likely Follow-Up Questions & Answers

**Q: Who else is doing this globally?**
"There are generic escrow platforms, but none built for the global informal service economy. None with portable Trust Scores. None with halal-compliant options. None priced accessibly. We're category creators, not category participants."

**Q: Why won't global payment processors just add this?**
"They're payment rails, not trust rails. Their moat is transaction volume, not relationship verification. By the time they build this, we'll have the data network effect — more businesses means more trust data, which means better scores, which means more businesses. That's defensible."

**Q: How do you get the first 100 customers?**
"Direct sales: live sellers, real estate agencies, restaurant associations. We're doing 10 free pilots right now with businesses in multiple US cities. Each pilot produces a testimonial. Zero ad spend, pure word-of-mouth."

**Q: What's your competition globally?**
"Depositly in Canada, Placepass in the US — but all for formal markets. The global informal service economy represents trillions in annual transactions and is completely unserved. We're starting in the US and expanding globally — same problem, same blueprint."

**Q: How do you prevent fraud?**
"Two-way scoring: businesses get scored too. Zero-knowledge proofs for sensitive data. Users control what they share. And our halal staking model means bad actors lose their stake if they cancel maliciously."

**Q: Why not crypto?**
"Two reasons: crypto isn't accessible for most small business owners globally, and it's too volatile. Pabandi Points are stable, compliant, and don't require a PhD to understand."

## Tone Guidelines

- **Voice:** Confident, warm, professional English suitable for global/US audiences.
- **Pace:** Measured. Don't rush. Leave 1-second pauses after big statements.
- **Emphasis:** "Trust," "Portable," "$29/month," "No credit card," "Zero fraud cost."
- **Avoid:** Jargon like "disruption," "synergy," "blockchain" (unless they ask).
- **Phrases to use:** "Pehle payment, phir baat," "One desk, one ticket, one frustrated user at a time."
