# NotebookLM Prompt: Fiza's Phantom Reservations Video Overview

## Role
You are generating a 30-second funny video overview audio track. The host is **Auntie Fiza**, a salon owner from Karachi with a thick South Asian/Desi accent.

## Accent & Delivery Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
- **South Asian accent**: Soft dental "t" and "d", sing-song intonation, Desi stress patterns on English words. Think: Karachi aunty explaining something very dramatic to her best friend.
- **Sarcastic tone**: Every other line should have at least a 70% sarcasm load. She's been betrayed by restaurants one too many times.
- **SLOW TEMPO for punchlines**: When Fiza delivers a sarcastic one-liner, slow the speaking rate by ~15%. Add a 0.5-second pause AFTER each punchline before the next line. The comedy comes from the slow burn, not the speed.
- **Energy arc**: Starts annoyed (0:00-0:07), escalates to dramatic betrayal (0:07-0:14), peaks at world-weary sarcasm (0:14-0:22), ends triumphant (0:22-0:30).

## Script Timing — 30 SECONDS EXACTLY

### 0:00-0:07 — The Setup
Fiza stands outside a fancy Karachi restaurant, full glam, on her phone.
Fiza (measured, slightly annoyed): "Yes, table for 4, under name Fiza. 8 PM. The name is Fiza. With a Z. Like the drink, but with less ice and more attitude."
[Background: Dramatic Bollywood strings, Karachi traffic]

### 0:07-0:14 — The Arrival
Cut inside. Restaurant is HALF EMPTY. Hostess says "system shows taken."
Fiza (SLOW TEMPO HERE, deadly calm): "Beta. Mera reservation hai. 8 PM. Ab batao. Kya hua?"
Hostess (nervous, fast): "I'm so sorry, there's been a mix-up, the system shows your table is... actually... taken."
Fiza (slowing down even more, horrified drawl): "Taken. Let me understand this. I called yesterday. I confirmed today. I wore my good jhumkas. And you're telling me my table is... taken."
[Text overlay: GHULAAM HAI KYA? / 15 KHALI TABLES]

### 0:14-0:22 — The Reveal
Fiza spots 15 empty tables. A group of 12 uncles walks in unannounced and gets seated immediately.
Fiza (WHISPER-SLOW, maximum sarcasm, each word deliberate): "Dekho beta. Wahan 15 khali table hain. Lekin unke liye jagah hai. Kyun? Kyunke unke paas connections hain. Mere paas sirf reservation hai. Aur pichhle baar ke biryani ka bill bhi pending hai."
[Background: Crickets chirping, sad trombone. Hold 2 seconds of silence after this line.]

### 0:22-0:30 — The Solution
Pabandi logo SLAMS in. Upbeat music drops. Fiza smirks at her phone showing "Deposit Received."
Fiza (picking up energy, triumphant): "Ab jab bhi koi restaurant book kare, pehle deposit maangta hun. Pehle payment, phir baat. No show? Thank you for the free biryani money. One desk, one ticket, one frustrated user at a time."
Voiceover (fast infomercial overlap): "Tired of phantom reservations? Pabandi. Deposit. Confirm. Show up. Or don't — we'll keep the money."
[Text overlay: PABANDI — Pehle Payment, Phir Baat]

## MUST Include (minimum 3)
1. "Pehle payment, phir baat"
2. "One desk, one ticket, one frustrated user at a time"
3. "Bacteria ki staging nahi, emotional staging hai" (Fiza's reaction to "system shows taken")

## South Asian Accent Specifics
- Soft "t" and "d": make "table", "taken", "today" sound softer than American English
- Stress shift: res-er-VA-tion, connec-TIONS, de-POS-it
- Intonation: slight rise at ends of questions
- Filler words allowed: "acha", "beta", "bas", "dekho", "biryani"
- Roman Urdu stay Roman: "beta", "khali", "dekho", "pichhle baar"

## Tempo Control Summary
- 0:00-0:07: Normal pace, building annoyance
- 0:07-0:14: Slowing down. Each sentence heavier than the last.
- 0:14-0:22: MINIMUM SPEED. Maximum sarcasm. This is the core joke. Let it breathe.
- 0:22-0:30: Picking up for the CTA, but still measured. Not rushed.
